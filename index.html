Frequently Asked Questions

How does WastedMeter calculate my wasted time?

WastedMeter takes your self-reported habits — screen time, commuting, social media, late nights — multiplies them across the years you've had those habits, and gives you a realistic estimate of how many hours have slipped by. It's not scientific, but it's honest.

How much time does the average person waste per day?

Studies show the average person spends 6–7 hours per day on TV and social media combined. Add commuting (avg. 1 hour/day), poor sleep habits, and unproductive routines — and most people waste 8–10 hours daily on low-value activities.

How much time does the average person spend on social media?

The global average is approximately 2 hours 27 minutes per day (DataReportal 2025). Over 10 years, that's roughly 3.7 years of waking life spent scrolling.

Is WastedMeter accurate?

WastedMeter is a reflective tool, not a precise tracker. The goal isn't perfect accuracy — it's to make the invisible visible. Seeing your habits expressed in years instead of hours tends to change your perspective.

What can I do after seeing my score?

Start with one habit. If you cut 1 hour of daily social media, you reclaim 15 full days per year. The calculator is a mirror — what you do with the reflection is up to you.
